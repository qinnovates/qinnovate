About the Neural Atlas

Qinnovate Neural Atlas is a professional standards body, research initiative, and specialized auditing organization dedicated to the governance of neurotechnology. We develop the security frameworks, validation protocols, and specialized auditing methodologies that define trustworthiness at the bio-digital boundary.

What We Build

Three interlocking standards that together define how neurotechnology should be secured, validated, and governed:

◆

QIF Model

The governance architecture

The OSI of Mind. An 11-band hourglass model mapping every surface — from neural tissue to synthetic systems — where security threats and ethical risks converge. One auditable framework for both.
▲

TARA

The dual-use atlas

A systematic classification of BCI techniques, mapping every attack to its therapeutic analog. Includes the Neural Impact Chain and DSM-5-TR diagnostic mapping.
■

NSP

The wire protocol

An RFC-style post-quantum protocol ensuring patient safety and data privacy — securing BCI data links with five defense layers at 3.25% power overhead.

Mission

Brain-computer interfaces are advancing faster than the security standards to protect them. Neuralink has implanted its N1 chip in human patients. Synchron's Stentrode is in FDA-approved clinical trials. Blackrock Neurotech has shipped Utah arrays for over a decade. Commercial EEG headsets from Emotiv, Muse, and OpenBCI sit on consumer shelves. These devices are tested for safety and efficacy. There is no standardized public framework to validate their resistance to adversarial threats. No standardized framework exists to classify, score, or mitigate attacks on the human brain.

Qinnovate exists to close that gap, developing the security specifications, threat models, and scoring metrics that the BCI industry needs before these devices reach mass adoption.

The Framework

QIF is an open, peer-reviewable security standard. It draws on three disciplines:

  • Cybersecurity — Layered defense models, threat taxonomies, attack surface analysis
  • Neuroscience — Neural signaling, coherence, scale-frequency invariants
  • Quantum physics — Decoherence, tunneling, entanglement as security primitives

The result is the Hourglass Model: an 11-band, 3-zone architecture with the Neural Interface (the physical BCI device) at its waist. Biology above. Synthetic below. Every band has defined threat vectors, coherence thresholds, and mitigation specifications.

Approach

Qinnovate follows the standards-body model: open publication, rigorous peer review, transparent methodology. All framework documents, equations, and reference implementations are publicly available.

We believe neural security standards must be developed openly — not behind closed doors. The people whose brains will be connected deserve to understand exactly how they're protected.

Founded By

Qinnovate was founded by Kevin Qi, a security researcher working at the intersection of cybersecurity, neuroscience, and quantum computing. Kevin developed QIF from first principles, beginning with a simple question: what does the OSI model look like when one endpoint is a brain?

Milestones

QIF was derived from first principles in 7 days of formal derivation — 50 log entries, 4 framework versions, from zero techniques to a complete stack. Every breakthrough, every dead end, every pivot is documented.
