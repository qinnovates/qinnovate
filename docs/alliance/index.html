Mission

Why a Standards Body?

In the 1960s, IT auditing didn't exist. Then ISACA and other professional standards bodies created the frameworks — COBIT, CISA, CRISC — that defined how organizations govern technology risk. Today, every Fortune 500 company relies on those standards.

Brain-computer interfaces are at that same inflection point. Neuralink, Synchron, and Blackrock Neurotech are moving from trials to market. But there is no equivalent for the brain. No professional body defining what "secure" means for a device that reads your thoughts.

The Neural Atlas fills that gap. We develop the risk frameworks, threat taxonomies, audit controls, and professional certifications that will define trustworthiness for neural interfaces — before regulators are forced to improvise.

Independence

The Alliance is vendor-neutral. We don't build BCI hardware — we build the frameworks that make hardware safe.

Transparency

All core standards are published under open licenses. Governance decisions are documented and publicly auditable.

Rigor

Every threat technique is scored, every control is testable, every claim is traceable to evidence.

Dual-Use Awareness

Every attack vector maps to a therapeutic analog. Security and clinical care are inseparable at the neural interface.
